## Observations

1. **Full vertical integration** — Minstar sells everything from raw precursors (Olivetol) to finished psychoactive products (THC analogues, DPT). This is a complete research chemical supply chain.

2. **Tryptamines are not cannabis** — The presence of DPT, MIPT, and other tryptamines under a "Cannabis" category suggests broad categorization for all "research chemicals" with psychoactive potential.

3. **The Retatrutide connection** — Minstar's core business model is sourcing and distributing chemicals, not manufacturing them. Their peptide offering (Retatrutide) comes from the same supplier network as their cannabinoids.

4. **US FDA Registration** — Despite being a research chemical distributor, they hold US FDA Registration No. 10249312842. This registration doesn't mean FDA approval of products — it's facility registration for import/export.

5. **DPT/MIPT are Schedule I** — In the US, these are Schedule I controlled substances. In China, tryptamines fall under varying regulatory control. Their open listing suggests internal Chinese regulatory gaps for novel psychedelics.
